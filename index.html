LonelyLessAustralia Decision Aid Tool

Loneliness affects over five million Australians, particularly older adults, causing significant health and economic challenges. This tool uses discrete choice experiment estimates to predict programme uptake probabilities, enabling personalized services based on the attributes older adults value most.

Use the tabs above to navigate the tool:

  • Instructions – Step-by-step guidance.
  • Attributes – Detailed explanation of programme features (hover over icons for extra info).
  • Inputs – Configure scenario parameters.
  • WTP Results – Visualize willingness-to-pay estimates with error bars.
  • Uptake – View predicted programme uptake probability.
  • Costs & Benefits – Analyze cost components and QALY-based benefits with icons and graphs.
  • Scenarios – Save, compare, and export different configurations.

How to Use This Tool

  1. Review the Attributes tab to understand each programme feature. Hover over the icons for additional explanations.
  2. Configure your scenario in the Inputs tab by selecting options such as state, cost adjustments, and service delivery methods.
  3. Click “Calculate & View Results” to generate updated charts and cost-benefit analyses.
  4. Examine the WTP Results tab to see how much value respondents assign to each attribute.
  5. View predicted participation in the Uptake tab.
  6. Review detailed cost breakdowns and benefits in the Costs & Benefits tab.
  7. Save and compare multiple scenarios in the Scenarios tab. You can also export results to PDF.

Programme Attributes

The following attributes influence how programmes are valued. Detailed descriptions are provided to help you understand each factor.

  • Cost per session: The out-of-pocket fee (AUD) required per attendance.
  • Accessibility: Determines if the programme is offered locally or in a wider community.
  • Frequency of Interaction: Indicates whether sessions are held weekly or monthly.
  • Method of Interaction: Choice between virtual and hybrid (in-person & virtual) formats.
  • Duration of Each Interaction: Options are 2-hour or 4-hour sessions.
  • Type of Support Programme: Options include community engagement, psychological counselling, or VR-based support.

Willingness to Pay (WTP) Results

WTP is derived from the ratio of each attribute’s coefficient to the negative cost coefficient. This quantifies the monetary value respondents assign to each feature.
