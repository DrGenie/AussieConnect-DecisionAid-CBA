Literature suggests small but meaningful health-related quality-of-life gains from structured loneliness interventions, often around 0.02–0.05 QALY per year, depending on baseline severity and intervention intensity.1–3

A value of 1.0 uses the DCE-estimated WTP directly. Lower values (e.g. 0.7) can be used if you prefer a conservative adjustment relative to typical income levels and out-of-pocket capacity of older adults in Australia.4,5

Discount rates of 3–5% are commonly used in health economic evaluations in Australia.6,7

Predicted endorsement (%)

Endorsement is defined as the share of older adults who would choose each program rather than no program, based on the ECL model. It is converted into an endorsement rate relative to the baseline opt-out option.

Average WTP per participant per year (AUD)

WTP is calculated from the DCE coefficients, converted to WTP per session, aggregated over the expected number of sessions per engaged participant and adjusted using the WTP scale factor.

QALY gains are calculated as endorsement-adjusted engagement multiplied by the annual QALY gain per engaged participant and aggregated over the time horizon with discounting. Cost per QALY can be compared with Australian benchmarks or thresholds.6,7

WTP-based net benefits (discounted)

Scenario PV WTP benefits (AUD) PV costs (AUD) Net benefit (AUD) BCR (WTP / cost)

WTP-based benefits represent the monetised value that older adults attach to each program relative to the baseline, using the ECL model. These benefits are conceptualised as a willingness-to-contribute measure rather than direct financial savings.
